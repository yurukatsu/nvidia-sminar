## Abstract

We apply sheaf neural networks, a generalization of graph neural networks (GNNs), to model commodities and capture cross-curve, cross-sector, and macro linkages. These models are used to generate probabilistic forecasts of returns, volatility, and co-movements. The resulting signals can then be combined with quantum-based portfolio optimization techniques to explore risk–return trade-offs.

## Topics

Data Science - Time Series Forecasting

## Key Takeaways:

- Understanding the mathematical foundations, using category theory and sheaves to model market structure and combine local information into a globally consistent market view
- Training sheaf neural networks to represent commodities and learn patterns in returns, volatility, and co-movements
- Exploring portfolio implementation using quantum-based optimization techniques to support risk, scenario, and allocation decisions
- Application of this framework to investment and portfolio management
